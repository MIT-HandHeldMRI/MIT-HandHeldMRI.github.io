6.4800: Biomedical Imaging with MRI

From Technology to Computation to Applications

Course Description

Medical imaging with MRI motivated by examples of problems in human health, that engage students in imaging hardware design, data acquisition and image reconstruction, signal analysis and inference. Data from scientific and clinical applications in neuro- and cardiac MRI as applied in current practice are sourced for computational labs.

Labs include kits for interactive and portable low-cost devices that can be assembled by the students to demonstrate fundamental building blocks of an MRI system. Lab MRI systems will be programmed by students for data collection and image reconstruction on student laptops.

Students apply concepts from lectures in labs for data collection for image reconstruction, image analysis, and inference by their own design, drawing on concepts in signal processing and machine learning.

Key Learning Areas

  • MRI hardware design principles
  • Data acquisition techniques
  • Image reconstruction algorithms
  • Signal analysis and inference
  • Applications in neuro- and cardiac MRI
  • Hands-on experience with MRI system components

Course Structure

This course combines theoretical lectures with practical lab sessions. Students will work with actual MRI data and build functioning components of MRI systems to gain hands-on experience.
